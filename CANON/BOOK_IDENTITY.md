# Book Identity

## Core Information

**Book Number:** 101
**Title:** 101 Ways to Die in a Hostel: Local Law
**Slug:** local-law
**Category:** Circumstantial

## The Death (One Sentence)
An arrogant Australian backpacker unknowingly becomes a drug mule in Kuala Lumpur, triggering Malaysia's mandatory death penalty for trafficking, and is killed in police custody before trial when the trafficking ring silences him.

## Tagline
"Ignorance of the law is no excuse. Especially when the penalty is death."

## Themes
1. **Primary:** Western entitlement and the delusion that privilege travels - satirizes the backpacker who treats developing nations as playgrounds with no consequences
2. **Secondary:** The invisible machinery of exploitation - how trafficking rings use naive travelers as disposable tools
3. **Tertiary:** The gap between curated travel identity (Instagram, blogs) and the grim reality of the places being "experienced"

## Tone Balance
- Comedy: 35%
- Horror: 40%
- Satire: 25%

## Target Reader
The reader who has stayed in hostels, who recognizes the type of traveler who refuses to learn the local language, who has watched someone confidently do something dangerously stupid abroad and thought "that's going to end badly." Anyone who has ever Googled "drug laws in [country]" before packing.

## Comparison Titles
- *The Beach* by Alex Garland meets *Final Destination* meets *An Idiot Abroad*

## Content Warnings
- Drug trafficking and drug culture
- Death in police custody
- Violence and murder staged as suicide
- Cultural insensitivity (satirized, not endorsed)
- Brief depictions of police corruption
- Depictions of the death penalty system
