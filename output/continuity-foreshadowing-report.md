## ADDITIONAL FORESHADOWING ELEMENTS

### The Dream Sequence (Ch 8)
Not listed in the foreshadowing checklist but one of the most powerful foreshadowing devices in the novel. Connor dreams of a grey room, a fluorescent light, the towel constricting in his hands, a presence behind him. This directly prefigures the holding cell, the murder, and Rizal's approach from behind. The dream is never remembered by Connor (line 373: "He would remember nothing"), making it a reader-only clue.

### David's Story (Ch 13)
David, an Australian man at the hostel bar, tells Connor his brother's story -- arrested on the North-South Expressway heading to Penang, with methamphetamine in a suitcase lining, now on death row in Kajang. This is a near-exact preview of Connor's fate, delivered by a man whose brother went through the identical experience. Connor changes the subject to beer.

### Felix at Wei's Shop (Ch 9)
Connor sees Felix inside Wei's phone repair shop at night, "talking to Wei... looking at something on a table." Connor waves; Felix doesn't wave back. This subtly establishes the Felix-Wei operational connection that becomes critical later.

### The Hostel's History (Ch 9)
Razak's stories of the hostel during Japanese occupation, as gambling den, as brothel. "Every use leaves ghosts." "The building has a hunger." This establishes the hostel as an entity that consumes its guests -- a metaphorical foreshadowing of the literal consumption of Connor by the trafficking network.

### The Bed 7 Cycle (Ch 48, 50)
In the aftermath, new guests check into Bed 7. Ch 48: Oliver sleeps in Bed 7, LENA's name (and others) still carved in the frame. Ch 50: Another new guest arrives. The cycle continues. This retroactively reframes all of Part 1's foreshadowing as part of a repeating pattern.

## GAPS AND ISSUES

### Minor Issue 1: "Joke Poster" Not Implemented as Specified
**DEATH_SCENARIO.md Hint 2** specifies: "A sign in the hostel lists 'Things That Can Get You Killed in Malaysia' as a joke poster -- Connor laughs at it without reading the details."

This specific element (a humorous poster listing dangerous things) does not appear in any chapter. Instead, the hostel warning is delivered through the house rules sheet (Ch 1, line 138-140) and the airport DADAH sign. The chapter plan marks this as complete with the notation "the red rule about Malaysian law." The intent is fulfilled through different means, but the specific comedic element of a joke poster is absent.

**Severity: LOW.** The alternative approach (serious-toned legal warnings that Connor ignores) may actually be more effective than a joke poster, as it makes Connor's ignorance more damning.

### Minor Issue 2: Tomoko's News Article Warning
**DEATH_SCENARIO.md Warning 2** specifies: "Tomoko shows Connor a news article about a foreigner executed for trafficking."

This specific scene does not occur. Tomoko shows Connor photographs of Felix (Ch 16), and Astrid (a fellow traveler) mentions executed foreigners in Ch 12. The warning about foreign executions reaches Connor but through a different character and in a different format.

**Severity: LOW.** The information reaches Connor through multiple channels, which is arguably more realistic. The spirit of the warning is preserved.

### Minor Issue 3: Lena's Name -- Surname Inconsistency
Lena's surname varies across chapters:
- Ch 24: "Lena Eriksson"
- Ch 25: "Lena Ekstrom"
- Ch 29: Not specified (just "Lena")
- Ch 47: "Lena Johansson"

This is a **continuity error**, not a foreshadowing issue. The character's surname changes three times. This should be flagged for the continuity pass.

**Severity: MEDIUM.** This will be noticed by attentive readers. A single surname should be standardized across all chapters.
